# Season Practice Outlines: MSB 341 Product Management (Fall 2026)

The season skeleton: one outline per practice, in order. Each gives the emphasis, the opener (a scripture and a quote tied to that emphasis, per the Wooden/Maeser model), the coach's story slot, the season ritual that runs, and the segment focus.

This is the pre-season plan, not the finished plan. Write the full minute-by-minute the week of, from `practice-plan-template.md`. Pull the coach's story from `story-catalog.md`. Season benchmarks (sprints, quizzes, peer work) are the source of truth in `00-schedule.qmd`; if a date here disagrees with the schedule, the schedule wins.

The openers below extend the truth-seeking opener bank in `practice-plan-template.md` across the whole season. The thread is product management as truth-seeking: Aim well, get evidence, believe in proportion to it, and act. Swap any pairing for one that fits the room that week.

Every practice ends with the huddle: two or three students show their deploy URL to the room. Small, frequent, low-stakes exposure to each other's work is the main peer-network mechanism, and it feeds the scrimmages.

## Practice 3 · Mon, Sep 28 · Aim

- **Sprint / phase:** Aim. Sprint 1 due, Sprint 2 assigned.

- **Scripture:** Matthew 7:20, "by their fruits ye shall know them." Judge a product by its effect on the people who use it.
- **Quote:** "Make something people want." (Paul Graham, Y Combinator) Aim it at a real problem, and want what is genuinely good for them.
- **Coach's story:** a time a good outcome hid a bad decision, or a product that worked but should not have shipped.
- **Season ritual:** huddle. First scrimmage-style peer look at Sprint 1 portfolios.
- **Film:** a shipped Sprint 1 portfolio from a prior cohort, read for client-ready polish.
- **Install:** product morality (impact on users, agency/becoming/connection) and problems worth solving (frustration and delight, conviction).
- **Drill:** React basics, Claude Code slash commands.
- **Reading / quiz:** Q3, Product Morality and Problems Worth Solving.

## Practice 4 · Mon, Oct 5 · Discover

- **Sprint / phase:** Discover. Sprint 2 in flight.
- **Emphasis:** Your assumptions are beliefs, not facts. Go get evidence. Ask about past behavior, not hypotheticals.
- **Scripture:** 1 Corinthians 13:12, "For now we see through a glass, darkly." A hypothetical answer is a smudge on the glass; past behavior wipes it clean.
- **Quote:** "Truth, or more precisely an accurate understanding of reality, is the essential foundation for producing good outcomes." (Ray Dalio, *Principles*)
- **Coach's story:** a time good information changed your mind (truth-seeking, story catalog).
- **Season ritual:** students share the "unfair advantage" they are surfacing in Sprint 2, so the cohort hears where each person's edge comes from.
- **Film:** a real customer-interview clip, mark where the interviewer led the witness.
- **Install:** problem discovery, Jobs-to-be-Done, the Mom Test.
- **Drill:** client-side state, forms, Claude Code agents.
- **Reading / quiz:** Q4, Problem Discovery.

## Practice 6 · Mon, Oct 19 · Build

- **Sprint / phase:** Build. Sprint 3 in flight.
- **Emphasis:** Ruthless scope. An MVP is a thin slice through every layer for the one core action, not a complete product.
- **Scripture:** Zechariah 4:10, "for who hath despised the day of small things?" Small, shipped, and real beats large and imagined.
- **Quote:** "If you're not embarrassed by the first version of your product, you've launched too late." (Reid Hoffman)
- **Coach's story:** a product that shipped too big and too late.
- **Season ritual:** huddle.
- **Film:** an MVP that tried to build everything, next to one that shipped the core action first.
- **Install:** MVP scoping, user stories, AI-assisted PRDs, MoSCoW.
- **Drill:** Supabase database, CRUD, row-level security.
- **Reading / quiz:** Q6, Building Your MVP.

## Practice 7 · Mon, Oct 26 · Build

- **Sprint / phase:** Build. Sprint 3 in flight. Mid-semester survey due.

- **Scripture / parable:** the six men of Indostan and the elephant (John Godfrey Saxe), "each was partly in the right, and all were in the wrong." One user is one blind man on the elephant; triangulate.
- **Quote:** "The truth must dazzle gradually, else every man be blind." (Emily Dickinson) Three to five tests start to reveal the shape.
- **Coach's story:** OCC, regulatory research. Makes an abstract "regulatory blocker" or platform constraint concrete (story catalog).
- **Season ritual:** huddle.
- **Film:** a usability-test recording, count how often the builder wanted to jump in and help.
- **Install:** usability testing (the curse of knowledge, severity levels) and platform strategy (web vs mobile, the platform spectrum).
- **Drill:** APIs and integrations, Claude Code hooks and skills.
- **Reading / quiz:** Q7, Testing with Users and Platform Strategy.

## Practice 8 · Mon, Nov 2 · Build

- **Sprint / phase:** Build into Grow. Sprint 3 due, Peer Code Review assigned.
- **Emphasis:** Keep what you built healthy. A product you cannot safely change is already dying.
- **Scripture:** Matthew 7:24-25, the house built upon a rock that stood when the rains came. Build on a foundation that holds up under load.
- **Quote:** "Always leave the code cleaner than you found it." (the campground rule, after Robert C. Martin)
- **Coach's story:** a codebase nobody could change, and what it cost.
- **Season ritual:** huddle. Preview the Peer Code Review: you will read a classmate's repo next week.
- **Film:** a diff that made the code worse while adding a feature, and the small refactor that would have prevented it.
- **Install:** sustainable code: readability, tests, refactoring, reviewing your own diffs with Claude Code.
- **Drill:** refactor one messy part of your product, add a test, review the diff before you commit.
- **Reading / quiz:** Q8, Sustainable Code.

## Practice 9 · Mon, Nov 9 · Grow

- **Sprint / phase:** Grow. Peer Code Review due, Sprint 4 assigned.
- **Emphasis:** Do not confuse a good outcome with a good decision. Read the metric as evidence, not a verdict.
- **Scripture:** John 8:32, "and ye shall know the truth, and the truth shall make you free." A metric is a way to know the truth about your product.
- **Quote:** "The quality of your decisions and the quality of your results are not perfectly correlated." (on *resulting*, after Annie Duke)
- **Coach's story:** McKinsey, analytics and performance management. The strongest single map in the catalog: the metrics hierarchy, leading vs lagging.
- **Season ritual:** huddle.
- **Film:** a dashboard full of vanity metrics next to one leading indicator that actually predicted retention.
- **Install:** product metrics, leading vs lagging indicators, the metrics hierarchy.
- **Drill:** analytics setup, event tracking.
- **Reading / quiz:** Q9, Measuring What Matters.

- **Sprint / phase:** Scrimmage. Sprint 4 due, Sprint 5 assigned. Withdraw deadline is Tue, Nov 24.

- **Scripture:** Proverbs 27:17, "iron sharpeneth iron; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend." The scrimmage is the sharpening.

- **Film:** none. Live demos are the film.
- **Install:** brief. How to give and take demo feedback well.
- **Drill:** build on the most useful feedback, group nearby to react.
- **Reading / quiz:** none.

- **Scripture:** Matthew 5:16, "let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works." Tell the story so the work is seen.

- **Install:** storytelling and the final demo: the five-second moment, show change, rider and elephant.
