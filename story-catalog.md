# Story Catalog (Instructor)

Your personal story shelf for teaching. Same idea students build in the [storytelling chapter](12-final-presentations.qmd): a stocked set of stories you can reach for on purpose, each tied to where it lands. Fill entries the week you plan to use them, or bank them whenever one comes to mind. Pull one into the "Coach's story" slot of a practice plan.

## How to log a story

Keep each entry short. The point is recall, not a script.

```
### [Title]
- Hook: the one line that opens it
- Setup: the situation, in two sentences
- The turn: the five-second moment the story pivots on
- The point: what it teaches (one sentence)
- Maps to: chapter / emphasis it supports
- Length: 30-second opener / 2-3 min chalk talk
- Source: McKinsey / OCC / Zions / Stanford / personal
```

## Placement map

Where your career naturally supplies a story, strongest fit first. Every chapter's "Why This Matters" opener is a ready slot for a one-paragraph "here's when this bit me in the real world."

| Experience | Best chapter / section | Why it lands |
|---|---|---|
| McKinsey, analytics + performance management | Ch 8 Measuring What Matters (the metrics hierarchy; leading vs lagging) | This was your job. Strongest single map. |
| McKinsey, principal product manager | Ch 1 (what a PM actually does) | Grounds "the CEO of the product" in a real day. |
| McKinsey, client-facing delivery | Sprint 1, "what client ready means" | "Client ready" is consulting language; a story of a deliverable that was not ready, and its cost. |
| Zions, VP building forecasting models | Ch 4 Validating Opportunities (opportunity scoring); Ch 10 (financial modeling) | Quantitative judgment under uncertainty. |
| OCC, regulatory research | Ch 7 Platform Strategy; Sprint 2 feasibility ("no regulatory blockers") | Makes an abstract "regulatory blocker" concrete. |
| Career arc broadly | Any chapter's "Why This Matters" opener | Each is a ready 60-second real-world hook. |
| Truth-seeking / decisions | Ch 3 discovery; the grading philosophy | A time good information changed your mind, or a good outcome hid a bad decision (resulting). |
